WeatherWear

Personalized clothing recommendation engine

Role Solo builder (PM + engineer)
Year 2025
Status Complete prototype
Stack React · JSX · Lucide

Problem

Weather apps tell you the temperature. None of them tell you what to wear. And everyone's body is different — 45°F feels like two different things to someone who runs cold vs. someone who runs hot, or a 65-year-old vs. a 28-year-old.

No tool personalizes clothing recommendations to the actual person, not just the weather.

How the engine works

Six environmental signals extracted from weather input, each normalized 0–1:

Signal What it captures
Cold Stress Cold protection need — peaks below -20°F, zero above 65°F
Heat Stress Cooling need — zero below 70°F, peaks above 110°F
Wind Exposure Wind chill risk, amplified when cold stress is high
Precipitation Snow = 1.0, heavy rain = 0.85, light rain = 0.5
UV Exposure Sun protection need on 0–11 UV index scale
Humidity Discomfort Muggy conditions — only activates when it's also hot

User profile adjusts every signal:

  • Thermal tendency (runs cold / neutral / runs hot): ±5°F offset on effective temperature
  • Age bracket (under 30 → over 65): increases cold and heat sensitivity with age
  • Activity level (sedentary / moderate / very active): active users generate body heat, reducing cold stress

Dynamic weights: signals that are active get amplified. Signals at zero get dampened but never fully excluded — prevents blind spots. A 30% bias cap prevents any single feature from dominating. Excess weight redistributes proportionally via iterative loop.

Scoring: weighted dot product of signal × weight × item_relevance across 67 clothing items (35 everyday, 32 athletic). Items below a 0.12 score threshold are excluded. The rest are ranked and labeled Essential / Recommended / Optional.

Key Decisions

No API key required

Manual sliders plus 8 city presets. Portable and demo-ready without backend infrastructure. Works offline.

30% bias cap

Prevents a single extreme signal (e.g., a blizzard) from collapsing all recommendations to cold-weather gear only. Excess weight redistributes proportionally.

Feedback loop baked in

Each item can be rated: accurate, too warm, too cold, irrelevant. Ratings drive live precision/recall/F1 metrics and export as structured JSON training records.

Single file

The entire engine is one weatherwear.jsx file. No build step, no config, no external dependencies beyond React and Lucide.

What it demonstrates

  • Feature engineering for a personalization problem
  • Dynamic weighting with bias controls
  • Explainability: every recommendation shows its score, a color-coded feature contribution bar, and top driving factor
  • Feedback loop architected for model iteration and training data collection

Learnings

  • Dynamic weights matter more than the item database. Getting the weight redistribution logic right — bias cap plus iterative normalization — was the core engineering problem.
  • Effective temperature is a better signal than raw temperature for personalization. Profile adjustments are small (±5°F) but compound across multiple features.
  • Building the feedback and metrics panel upfront forced clarity on what "accurate" means for this kind of recommendation.
